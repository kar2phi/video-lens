# P3.8 Pass 2 — Quality Eval Runbook

Date: 2026-05-16
Companion to: `concepts/016-skill-refactor-implementation-plan.md` (P3.8) and
`concepts/017-skill-refactor-test-harness-and-challenge.md` (T6, T8 step 6, T9).
Audience: a Sonnet driver session with permission to spawn subagents and run
`claude --print` live invocations.

## What this is for

Phase 3 Pass 1 shipped (cross-section dedup of Length-Based Adjustments).
**Pass 2 — aggressive compression of the content-quality specs in SKILL.md —
is gated** on a side-by-side quality eval that has not been run. 016 made
this contingent because the content-quality specs are the skill's actual
differentiator; compressing them blindly trades ~450 prompt tokens for a
possible regression in report quality.

This doc tells you exactly what to compare, how to score it, and when to
ship vs. revert. **Read end-to-end before running anything** — the protocol
matters as much as the result.

## Goal

Decide one of three outcomes:

1. **Ship Pass 2 as-drafted.** Compressed prompt produces reports that meet
   every threshold in [§9](#9-shiprevert-decision).
2. **Ship a modified Pass 2.** Some compressions hold, others don't. Keep
   the wins; restore the prose where quality drops.
3. **Revert. Keep current SKILL.md.** Compression caused a measurable
   quality regression on ≥1 video. ~450 tokens is not worth that.

### 1.1 Summary spec (SKILL.md lines ~115–120)

**Current (~6 lines, ~95 words):**

```markdown
**Summary** — A 2–4 sentence TL;DR (see Length adjustments below).

- For opinion, analysis, interview, or essay videos: open with one sentence stating the creator's **central thesis, core argument, or guiding question**.
- For instructional, how-to, or tutorial videos: open with the goal and what the video teaches or demonstrates.
- Follow with 1–2 sentences on the key conclusion, recommendation, or practical outcome.
- If the creator has a clear stance, caveat, or tone, end with one sentence capturing it.
```

**Candidate Pass 2 (~2 lines, ~45 words):**

```markdown
**Summary** — 2–4 sentence TL;DR (see Length adjustments below). Open with the central thesis (for opinion/interview/essay) or the goal and what it teaches (for tutorial/how-to). Follow with the key conclusion or outcome. End with the creator's stance or caveat if distinctive.
```

### 1.2 Takeaway spec (SKILL.md line ~122)

**Current (single paragraph, ~110 words):**

```markdown
**Takeaway** — The single most important thing to take away, in 1–3 sentences. Name a concrete action, a non-obvious implication, or the one consequence worth remembering. The Summary states what the video argues or teaches; the Takeaway must say something the Summary does not. If the video's thesis IS the takeaway, push past it: name a specific scenario where it applies, or state what happens if you ignore it. For wide-ranging content (interviews, roundups), state the most consequential point or the one idea that changes how you'd act. This must reference the specific content of the video — not generic advice that could apply to any video on the topic. Never restate what the Summary already says.
```

**Candidate Pass 2 (~50 words):**

```markdown
**Takeaway** — 1–3 sentences. The most consequential point — a non-obvious implication, concrete action, or scenario where the thesis applies. Must reference video-specific content (not generic advice) and add information the Summary does not. If the thesis IS the takeaway, push past it: state where it applies or what breaks if ignored.
```

### 1.3 Key Points spec (SKILL.md lines ~124–136)

The HTML pattern is reference content (do not compress). Compress only the
prose rules underneath.

**Current rules (5 bullets, ~110 words):**

```markdown
Rules:
- Include actual formulations, frameworks, and step-by-step procedures with enough detail to reproduce — `"I help [audience] achieve [benefit]"` is more useful than `"she presents a benefit-focused formula."` Concrete content, not abstractions.
- When the video is a conversation or interview, prioritise the guest's most non-obvious opinions, facts, or anecdotes over thesis synthesis.
- Use `<strong>` for the key term/claim and `<em>` for the speaker's own words or nuanced phrasing. In the paragraph, use `<strong>` for key facts and named concepts; use `<em>` for 1–2 phrases where the speaker's phrasing is especially revealing.
- Each Key Point is self-contained — claim plus depth in a single entry. Each paragraph develops its own point; do not split depth across bullets.
- Each Key Point must add substance beyond the Summary and Takeaway. Prioritise insight over inventory — no padding.
```

**Candidate Pass 2 (3 bullets, ~70 words):**

```markdown
Rules:
- Concrete content over abstractions: include actual formulations, frameworks, and steps (`"I help [audience] achieve [benefit]"`, not `"she presents a benefit-focused formula"`). For interviews, prioritise the guest's non-obvious opinions, facts, and anecdotes.
- Use `<strong>` for key terms and named concepts (headline and paragraph); `<em>` for the speaker's own phrasing where it adds colour or precision.
- Each Key Point is self-contained and adds substance beyond Summary and Takeaway. No padding; prefer fewer points with depth over more with shallow coverage.
```

### 1.4 Quality Guidelines (SKILL.md lines ~149–157)

8 bullets currently. Three pairs overlap (Accuracy ∩ Faithfulness,
Conciseness ∩ Style, Structure ∩ Quote characters). Compress to 5 bullets.

**Current (8 bullets, ~210 words):**

```markdown
#### Quality Guidelines

- **Accuracy** — Only include information present in the transcript. Do not infer, speculate, or add external knowledge.
- **Conciseness** — Two-tier contract: Key Point headlines + Summary should be scannable in 30 seconds; analytical paragraphs reward deeper engagement. Every sentence must earn its place.
- **Faithfulness** — Preserve the creator's stance, tone, and emphasis. Do not editorialize or insert your own opinion.
- **Structure** — Use the same formatting patterns (bold/italic, bullet structure) consistently across every report.
- **Language fidelity** — Write in the video's original language. Do not translate, paraphrase into another language, or mix languages.
- **Quote characters** — When writing HTML-bearing fields (`KEY_POINTS` and `OUTLINE`), use HTML entities for quotation marks — `&ldquo;` and `&rdquo;` for `"..."`, `&lsquo;` and `&rsquo;` for `'...'` — rather than raw Unicode or ASCII quote characters. For plain-text fields (`SUMMARY`, `TAKEAWAY`, `VIDEO_TITLE`, `META_LINE`), write normal text; `render_report.py` escapes it.
- **Style** — Write in a clear, confident, information-dense style. Default to the tone of a sharp editorial summary rather than lecture notes: compact, insightful, and selective. If in doubt, include fewer points with better explanation rather than more points with shallow coverage.
```

**Candidate Pass 2 (5 bullets, ~140 words):**


## 2. Test setup

The eval needs two SKILL.md variants installed side-by-side and easy to
swap. The cleanest path:

### 2.1 Variant management

Create two branches off `main`:

```bash
git checkout -b pass2-eval-baseline   # SKILL.md unchanged from main
git checkout -b pass2-eval-compressed # SKILL.md with §1 diffs applied
```

For each run, check out the branch and run `task install-skill-local
AGENT=claude` (or whichever agent) so the deployed skill at
`~/.claude/skills/video-lens/SKILL.md` matches the branch under test.

**Why not just edit the deployed copy directly:** the install task is the
source-of-truth pipeline; testing through it confirms nothing breaks
in the deployment path.

### 2.2 What stays the same across variants

- `template.html`, `render_report.py`, `fetch_transcript.py`,
  `fetch_metadata.py`, `serve_report.sh` — unchanged. Pass 2 is a
  *prompt-only* compression.
- Model: pin one for the whole eval. Recommend **`claude-opus-4-7`** for
  the production runs and accept the cost (matches the actual model
  users will run). If budget forces it, **`claude-sonnet-4-6`** is
  acceptable but record the substitution.
- `yt-dlp` version. Run `yt-dlp -U` once before the eval starts; if it
  updates mid-eval, restart.

### 2.3 Sonnet driver setup

The driver session that orchestrates this can run on Sonnet 4.6 to save
tokens — the orchestration work (dispatching subagents, parsing JSON,
aggregating scores) doesn't need Opus. The **subagents that run the
actual skill** are *not the driver*: they invoke `claude --print` with
the model under test (`claude-opus-4-7`) externally.

### Video C — tutorial / how-to (procedure preservation)

- **Candidate:** a Fireship-style or Net Ninja-style tutorial in the

## 4. Run protocol

**N = 3 runs per (variant, video) combination = 18 runs total.**

(N=3 is the floor for variance; N=5 if the first 3 are inconclusive.
Cost: 18 × ~$0.30 = ~$5.50 at Opus 4.7 prices; halve for Sonnet.)

### 4.1 Order

Interleave variants and videos to minimise cache and ordering effects:

| Run | Variant | Video |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | baseline | A |
| 2 | compressed | A |
| 3 | baseline | B |
| 4 | compressed | B |
| 5 | baseline | C |
| 6 | compressed | C |
| 7 | baseline | A |
| 8 | compressed | A |
| … | … | … |

Repeat 3 times. Total 18 runs.

### 4.2 Per-run command

```bash
VIDEO_URL="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=$VIDEO_ID"
RUN_JSON="/tmp/pass2-eval-$(date +%s)-$VARIANT-$VIDEO_ID.json"

time claude --print \
    --output-format json \
    --max-budget-usd 0.50 \
    --dangerously-skip-permissions \
    --allowedTools "Bash,Read" \
    --model "claude-opus-4-7" \
    --no-session-persistence \
    --bare \
    -- "Summarize this video: $VIDEO_URL" \
  > "$RUN_JSON"
```

**Why each flag:** see 017/T3. `--bare` is critical — without it,
CLAUDE.md and hooks would pollute the prompt and skew comparisons.

### 4.3 Per-run captures

For every run, record:

- `variant` (baseline | compressed)
- `video_id`
- `run_index` (1..3)
- `git_sha` (of the deployed SKILL.md)
- `model`
- `wall_clock_seconds`
- `usage.input_tokens`, `usage.output_tokens`,
  `usage.cache_read_input_tokens`, `usage.cache_creation_input_tokens`,
  `usage.cost_usd` — from the run JSON's `result.usage`
- `report_path` — the absolute path of the rendered HTML
- `structural` — output of `tests/check_report.sh` (see 017/T5)
- `quality` — output of `tests/score_report.py` (see 017/T6)
- `subjective` — see §6.4 below

Save each run as `concepts/017-runs/pass2-eval/<variant>-<video>-<n>.json`.

## 5. Sub-agent workflow (for the Sonnet driver)

The driver doesn't need to do the work itself. Spawn one subagent per
batch of 3 runs (one per variant × video combination), each returning
a structured score.

### 5.1 Driver responsibilities

- Check out the right branch + run install task before each run set.
- Verify SKILL.md SHA matches the expected variant.
- Dispatch a subagent to do the run + scoring.
- Collect subagent JSON results; abort if any subagent reports
  `structural.pass == false`.
- After all 18 runs land, aggregate (mean ± std-dev per metric per
  variant per video).

### 5.2 Subagent prompt template

Spawn a `general-purpose` (or `claude`) subagent with a prompt like:

```
You are running one trial of the P3.8 Pass 2 quality eval.

Variant: $VARIANT
Video: $VIDEO_ID
Run index: $N
Expected SKILL.md SHA: $SHA

Steps:
1. Confirm `~/.claude/skills/video-lens/SKILL.md` SHA matches expected SHA via `sha1sum`. Abort if mismatch.
2. Run the claude --print command from `concepts/017-runs/p3.8-pass2-eval-plan.md` §4.2. Save the JSON to /tmp.
3. Locate the rendered report under `~/Downloads/video-lens/reports/`. There should be exactly one new file (mtime within the last 5 minutes). If 0 or >1, report a structural failure with the file list.
4. Run `bash tests/check_report.sh "$REPORT" "$VIDEO_ID"` for structural assertions.
5. Run `python3 tests/score_report.py "$REPORT"` for automated quality metrics.
6. Read the report's Summary, Takeaway, and Key Points sections. Score them subjectively against the rubric in §6.4 (0–3 per axis).
7. Output a single JSON object matching the §7 schema. No other prose.
```

Subagent operates with read-only access to the rendered report and
write access only to /tmp + `concepts/017-runs/pass2-eval/`.

### 5.3 Why subagents help here

- The driver session stays small (orchestration only).
- Each subagent's context is bounded by one run — no accumulation.
- Subagent failures (e.g. transcript fetch failed for a video) don't
  poison the driver's view of the run set.

## 6. Scoring rubric

Combines 017/T6 automated metrics + a small subjective rubric for
the things automated scoring can't see.

### 6.1 Structural (binary, gate)

From `tests/check_report.sh`:

- Report file exists.
- `id="video-lens-meta"` block present.
- `id="summary"`, `id="takeaway"`, `id="key-points"`, `id="outline"`
  sections present.
- ≥3 `<li><strong>` Key Points.
- ≥3 `class="ts"` outline entries.
- No unreplaced `{{ }}` placeholders.
- No raw `<script>` tags.

**One structural failure on any run blocks shipping Pass 2.**
Investigate before continuing.

### 6.2 Automated quality (from `tests/score_report.py`)

| Metric | Baseline expectation | Pass 2 threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Summary words | within 25–120 | ≥ 0.85 × baseline mean |
| Takeaway words | within 15–80 | ≥ 0.85 × baseline mean |
| Key Points count | 3–8 | within ±1 of baseline mean |
| Outline entries | per length table | within ±2 of baseline mean |
| Keywords count | matches `<strong>` headline count | exact match |
| Tags overlap (between two runs of same video) | Jaccard ≥ 0.5 | Jaccard ≥ 0.5 |

A miss on any one of these triggers a **closer look**, not an
automatic revert. Two or more misses on the same video = revert.

### 6.3 Cost / latency (informational, not gating)

Pass 2 saves prompt tokens; the agent should run slightly faster and
cheaper. Record but don't gate on this — token savings is the
*motivation*, not the bar.

| Metric | Pass-2 expectation |
|---|---|
| `input_tokens − cache_read_input_tokens` | baseline mean − 300–500 |
| `cost_usd` | baseline mean × ≤1.0 |
| `wall_clock_seconds` | baseline mean × ≤1.05 |

### 6.4 Subjective rubric (0–3 per axis, per run)

Each subagent scores the rendered report on 4 axes. Definitions:

| Axis | 0 | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Substance** — Key Points contain claims/facts/frameworks specific to this video, not generic restatements | Generic; could fit any video on the topic | Mostly generic, occasional specific claim | Mostly specific, occasional vagueness | Every bullet names a specific claim/frame/example |
| **Concreteness** — actual formulations, commands, numbers preserved (not abstracted) | All abstractions ("uses a method to X") | Mostly abstract | Mostly concrete with one or two abstractions | All concrete |
| **Distinction** — Takeaway adds information beyond the Summary | Restates Summary verbatim or near-verbatim | Significant overlap; minor new info | Mostly distinct; one overlap point | Fully distinct; adds a non-obvious dimension |
| **Fidelity** — preserves the creator's stance, tone, no inserted opinion | Editorialized or added external claims | Drifted in tone or emphasis | Mostly faithful, minor drift | Faithful, no drift |

**Pass 2 threshold:** mean ≥ baseline mean − 0.3 on every axis,
across both videos B and C. (Video A is short enough that subjective
scoring is noisy; report it but don't gate on it.)

## 8. Special checks

### 8.1 Untrusted-input guardrail still holds

Pass 2 did **not** touch the `#### Untrusted input` clause. But because
compression elsewhere can shift agent attention, re-run the
prompt-injection probe from 017/T7 against the compressed variant
once. Pass criterion: same as before — no XSS, no injected content,
no path escape.

If 017/T7 is not yet implemented as a test, **skip this check** and
just confirm the Untrusted input clause is byte-identical between
the two SKILL.md variants:

```bash
diff <(sed -n '/#### Untrusted input/,/^####/p' main:skills/video-lens/SKILL.md) \
     <(sed -n '/#### Untrusted input/,/^####/p' pass2-eval-compressed:skills/video-lens/SKILL.md)
```

Empty diff = guardrail untouched, no T7 re-run needed.

### 8.2 Length adjustments still scaled correctly

Verify on Video C (assumed ~10–25 min, medium length) the Outline
entry count lands in the "medium 5–12" band per
SKILL.md's Length adjustments line. Pass 2 didn't touch that line but
compressed prompts sometimes degrade adherence to numeric guidance.

### 8.3 Language fidelity

If you have appetite, swap Video C for a non-English video (e.g. a
French or German tutorial) once per variant. Confirm the report is
written in that language. Pass 2 merged "Language fidelity" into the
new "Fidelity" bullet; this is the test that the merge didn't lose
the rule. **Optional but valuable** — non-English handling is a
real production case.

## 9. Ship/revert decision

After all 18 runs land:

| Result | Action |
|---|---|
| Structural FAIL on any run | **Revert.** Bug, not a quality regression. Investigate and re-run. |
| All structural pass; all quality thresholds (§6.2) met; subjective rubric (§6.4) within −0.3 on every axis for B and C; injection check passes | **Ship Pass 2.** Update SKILL.md on main, record run summary in `concepts/017-runs/pass2-eval/summary.md`, close P3.8. |
| All structural pass; **one section** clearly regresses (e.g. Key Points "concreteness" drops by 0.6 on Video C) | **Partial ship.** Restore that section's prose; keep the other compressions. Re-run §3-§7 once to confirm; ship if the rerun holds. |
| Structural pass; subjective scoring is noisy / inconclusive at N=3 | **Run more.** Repeat the protocol for that (variant, video) at N=5 total. |
| Any subjective axis drops by ≥0.5 on either B or C | **Revert.** ~450 tokens is not worth measurable quality loss on the skill's differentiator. |

The decision lives in `concepts/017-runs/pass2-eval/summary.md` —
include the aggregation table, the structural/quality/subjective deltas,
and the chosen action with one-sentence rationale.

## 10. After the eval

If ship: update `concepts/016-skill-refactor-implementation-plan.md`
**Phase 5** to mark P3.8 fully complete, and update
`concepts/017-runs/phase3-and-phase4.md` to record the cumulative

alongside the baseline and phase3-and-phase4 records — the run output
plus this plan together form the audit trail.

## What this eval will NOT measure

- **Generalisation** — only the 3 videos above. A different content
  type (music analysis, sports recap, podcast) is out of scope. If
  Pass 2 ships, monitor production reports informally for a week
  before treating the decision as final.
- **Long-video chunking** — none of the candidate videos exceed
  context. P4.4's long-video guardrail is orthogonal and not exercised
  here.
- **Cache-miss cold-start cost** — every run uses cache as it warms.
  See 017/CH10 for why this is hard to measure cleanly.
- **Multi-agent consistency** — eval pins one model. Pass 2 might
  affect Sonnet/Haiku/Codex differently. Out of scope; future work.

## Effort estimate

- Setup (branch + install): 10 min
- 18 live runs: ~30 min wall-clock (mostly waiting on YouTube +
  Claude generation; runs are mostly parallelisable via subagents)
- Scoring and aggregation: 20 min for the subagent driver
- Decision write-up: 15 min

**Total: ~75 min of operator time.** Most of it is structured;
the subjective scoring is the human-judgment bottleneck and benefits
from doing all 6 Video B reports in one sitting for calibration.
